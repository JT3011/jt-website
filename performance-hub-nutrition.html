Your Nutrition Focus

Simple Performance Meals

Build Your Plate.

Food does not need to be complicated. Aim for regular meals, enough energy and a balanced mix of carbohydrates, protein, fruit, vegetables and fluids.

Training And Match Timing

Fuel At The Right Time.

Familiar foods usually work best before football. Avoid making match day the moment you experiment with something completely new.

01

Earlier In The Day

Eat a balanced meal containing carbohydrates, protein and familiar foods that feel comfortable.

02

Before Football

Choose an easy-to-digest snack when needed, such as fruit, toast, yoghurt or a simple cereal option.

03

During Activity

Drink regularly and use breaks as a reminder rather than waiting until you feel very thirsty.

04

After Football

Follow training or matches with fluids and a meal or snack containing carbohydrates and protein.

Hydration Habits

Arrive Ready.

Start Early Drink across the day rather than trying to catch up immediately before football.
Bring A Bottle Keep water available at school, work, training and matches.
Use Conditions Hot weather, long sessions and heavy sweating increase the importance of regular fluids.
Recover Afterwards Continue drinking after the session instead of stopping when football finishes.

Everyday Standards

Consistency Wins.

Eat Regularly Skipping meals can make it harder to train with energy and concentration.
Include Carbohydrates Foods such as rice, pasta, potatoes, bread, oats and fruit help provide energy for football.
Include Protein Foods such as eggs, yoghurt, milk, beans, fish, meat and tofu can support growth and recovery.
Choose Variety Use different fruits, vegetables and meal choices across the week rather than chasing one perfect food.

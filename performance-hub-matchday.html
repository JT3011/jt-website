Your Matchday Mission

Matchday Timeline

Prepare Without Panic.

Good preparation should feel calm and familiar. Avoid leaving food, kit, travel and mental preparation until the final minute.

01

Earlier.

Eat familiar meals, drink regularly and organise every item of kit before travel begins.

Check boots, shin pads, clothing, water and travel details.
02

Arrival.

Arrive with enough time to settle, change and understand the team plan without rushing.

Greet teammates, listen and begin switching attention to football.
03

Warm-Up.

Build temperature and intensity gradually before adding sharper, match-speed actions.

Finish feeling switched on—not tired before kick-off.
04

Kick-Off.

Keep the opening actions clear and simple. Communicate, scan and become involved immediately.

Focus on the first useful action rather than the perfect match.

Physical Preparation

Build Match Speed.

A warm-up should progress from easy movement towards football actions, changes of direction and short periods of match intensity.

Phase One

Raise Temperature.

Begin with relaxed movement and gradually increase your range and speed.

Easy jogging and movement in different directions
Dynamic ankle, hip and upper-body movements
Gradual accelerations rather than instant maximum sprints
Phase Two

Connect With The Ball.

Use simple football actions to sharpen touch, awareness and communication.

Clean passing with both feet
Scan before receiving
Move immediately after releasing the ball
Phase Three

Switch On.

Finish with a small number of sharp actions that match your position and role.

During The Match

Stay In The Game.

Every match contains mistakes, difficult periods and moments where confidence changes. The next useful action remains available.

Half-Time Reset

Review. Refuel. Refocus.

Settle First Slow your breathing and take fluids before analysing every moment of the first half.
Listen Properly Give full attention to the coach’s main tactical messages.
Choose One Adjustment Identify the most useful change for your position or role.
Restart Positively Enter the second half focused on the next action—not trapped in the first half.
The JT Match Reset

After A Mistake.

Recognise Notice frustration or disappointment without feeding it.
Release Use one controlled breath out and relax unnecessary tension.
Refocus Use your cue: “next action”, “stay available” or “be brave”.
Respond Move, communicate and complete the next useful action.
Post-Match Reflection

Learn Without Judging.

Avoid deciding the entire performance was brilliant or terrible. Use specific evidence to recognise progress and choose what comes next.

Your Mindset Mission

Mental Performance Training

Train The Mind.

Mindset is not about pretending everything feels easy. It is about noticing what is happening, controlling the next action and returning attention to what helps performance.

Match-Day Routine

Stay Present.

Use the same simple routine regularly. Familiar preparation can reduce unnecessary noise and help attention return to the game.

01

Before The Match

Choose one clear intention, such as communicating early, scanning regularly or attacking the first action positively.

02

First Five Minutes

Keep your decisions simple, communicate and focus on completing the next useful action rather than forcing something spectacular.

03

After A Mistake

Breathe out, use your reset word and move immediately into the best position for the next phase of play.

04

After The Match

Record one thing you did well, one lesson and one action to carry into your next training session.

The JT Reset

Control The Next Action.

Recognise Notice frustration, nerves or distraction without allowing it to control the next decision.
Release Use one slow breath out to reduce tension and interrupt the emotional reaction.
Refocus Use a short cue such as “next action”, “stay available” or “be brave”.
Respond Move, communicate and perform the next useful action with full commitment.
Your development goal:
Confidence Standards

Build Evidence.

Prepare Properly Confidence grows when you repeatedly complete the work you promised yourself you would do.
Use Helpful Language Speak to yourself with clear instructions rather than insults, panic or exaggerated criticism.
Accept Imperfection Strong players still make mistakes. The response is often more important than the mistake itself.
Track Progress Record small improvements so confidence is based on evidence, not only on how you feel that day.

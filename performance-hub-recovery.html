Your Recovery Mission

Recovery Foundations

Win The Basics.

Expensive gadgets cannot rescue poor habits. Consistent sleep, food, fluids and sensible movement remain the foundations of reliable recovery.

01

Sleep.

Keep a consistent bedtime, reduce late-night screen use and allow enough time for genuine rest.

Tonight: begin your wind-down routine at the same planned time.
02

Hydration.

Drink regularly across the day and continue replacing fluids after training or matches.

Today: keep a drink available rather than waiting until thirsty.
03

Nutrition.

Follow football with a balanced meal or snack containing carbohydrates, protein and fluids.

After football: refuel with familiar food as soon as practical.
04

Movement.

Gentle movement can help you feel less stiff, but recovery sessions should not become another exhausting workout.

Recovery day: choose relaxed walking, mobility or easy ball work.

Practical Recovery Routines

Choose The Right Reset.

Use the routine that matches your day. Keep recovery work calm, controlled and comfortable.

Immediately After Football

The First Hour.

Begin recovering before tiredness and poor planning take over.

Walk gently and allow breathing to settle
Replace fluids gradually
Eat a balanced meal or suitable snack
Change out of wet or cold kit
Evening Recovery

Eight-Minute Reset.

Use relaxed movement rather than forcing painful stretches.

60 seconds relaxed breathing
60 seconds ankle and calf movement
60 seconds hip rotations
60 seconds gentle hamstring movement
Repeat once without forcing range
The Following Day

Restore Movement.

Recovery days should leave you feeling better, not more tired.

Easy walk or relaxed cycle
Gentle mobility through comfortable ranges
Light ball touches when appropriate
Continue normal meals, fluids and sleep habits
Training Load Awareness

More Is Not Always Better.

Protect Quality Repeated poor-quality sessions can add fatigue without producing useful development.
Plan Hard And Easy Days Avoid making every day intense. Adaptation needs periods where the body is allowed to recover.
Speak Up Early Tell a parent, guardian or coach when unusual tiredness, pain or reduced performance continues.
Return Gradually After illness, injury or time away, rebuild training rather than immediately attempting your normal maximum workload.

Recovery Warning Signs

Know When To Stop.

Sharp Or Increasing Pain Stop the activity and tell a parent, guardian, coach or qualified healthcare professional.
Concussion Symptoms Headache, confusion, dizziness, nausea or unusual behaviour after a head impact require immediate adult attention.
Breathing Difficulty Or Chest Pain Stop immediately and seek urgent real-world support.
Persistent Exhaustion Ongoing extreme tiredness, illness or major performance changes should not be ignored.
Important: This page provides general recovery education and does not diagnose injuries or replace medical care. Young players should use the guidance with a parent, guardian or coach. Stop activity and seek appropriate professional support for pain, injury, concussion symptoms, breathing difficulty, dizziness or persistent concerns.
